Who is Thrive?

Thrive – the Community Council for Somerset – has been working across Somerset since 1926. It believes that everyone in the county should be able to live a healthy, independent and safe life, and be part of a strong and thriving community.

Thrive’s work is genuinely local. Teams of advocates work across every corner of Somerset – from village agents supporting older people in rural communities to diverse communities programmes, enterprise support, and campaigns on issues like fuel poverty. The organisation does not deliver services from a distance. It listens, responds and builds connection.

“I just want to say thank you to the village agents. My husband came out of hospital and received very little support, but when the village agents came, they got everything sorted. We wouldn’t have managed without them.”

This is also Thrive’s centenary year. After 100 years of serving Somerset’s communities, the organisation is not standing still – it is actively repositioning itself for the next chapter: more mission-focused, more advocacy-driven, and more deliberately building a movement of community supporters.

Why this role, why now?

Thrive is at a genuinely exciting inflection point. The new chief executive has reset the strategic direction. The centenary year is generating real community energy. And the organisation has made a clear decision: it wants to build a sustainable, diversified fundraising base – one that is not dependent on statutory contracts and grants alone.

This role is the cornerstone of that ambition. It is not a like-for-like replacement. It is a leadership position for someone who wants to build something – to create a community fundraising and individual giving programme, largely from scratch, in an organisation that has the mission, the reach and the stories to make it work.

The honest picture

Thrive is starting from a low base on individual and community fundraising. The tools are in place. The mission is compelling. The stories are extraordinary. What is missing is the right person to build the pipeline, grow the supporter base and turn centenary momentum into lasting income. That is what this role is about.

The centenary year alone offers a powerful platform – community parties across Somerset, a schools programme, a November ball, and a growing list of people who are beginning to engage with Thrive as supporters rather than simply as service users. The ambition is 2,000 active supporters by year end, with a clear pathway to converting them into regular givers over time.

What the role involves

This is a broad, hands-on role. The Fundraising Manager will lead across three income streams, with community fundraising and individual giving as the primary focus:

Community fundraising, events and individual giving

  • Building and running a structured community fundraising programme – creating frameworks that enable supporters, staff and volunteers to raise money on behalf of Thrive
  • Growing the individual giving base from a low starting point – developing supporter journeys, regular giving and stewardship
  • Making the case internally for paid digital acquisition campaigns and working closely with Thrive’s marketing team to implement them
  • Developing challenge events and community fundraising activity that reflects Thrive’s grassroots, place-based character

Trusts and foundations

  • Managing a grants pipeline and submitting funding applications to trusts and foundations
  • Writing compelling funding applications and funder reports, working with colleagues across the organisation to capture impact
  • Building and maintaining relationships with existing and prospective funders

Leadership and planning

  • Line managing the Fundraising Administrator – providing direction, support and development, and helping them build grant-writing skills over time
  • Contributing to fundraising planning and targets, and reporting progress clearly to the Head of Development and Communications
  • Helping build a fundraising culture across the organisation where frontline staff see themselves as part of the story

Who we are looking for

The right candidate will be someone for whom community engagement and individual giving feels natural – someone who is energised by building relationships, motivating supporters and growing a donor base, and who brings enough experience of trusts and foundations to hold that aspect of the role with confidence.

More specifically:

  • A track record in community fundraising, individual giving or events fundraising – this is the core of the role
  • Some experience of grant-writing and trusts and foundations fundraising – this does not need to be your primary specialism
  • Experience of managing or mentoring others
  • Strong written skills – the ability to translate real human stories into compelling fundraising copy
  • Genuine passion for the mission – Thrive’s frontline staff are deeply connected to the communities they serve, and they will know quickly whether a fundraiser shares that commitment
  • A self-starter who can work with autonomy in a small team environment
  • Based in Somerset or within easy reach of Taunton – the role is primarily home-based but requires approximately one day per week in the Taunton office
